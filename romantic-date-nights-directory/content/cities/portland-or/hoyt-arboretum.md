## A Living Forest Sanctuary Above the City

Tucked into the forested hills of Washington Park, Hoyt Arboretum offers something increasingly rare in modern romance — genuine stillness. More than 12 miles of winding trails thread through one of the most diverse tree collections in the country, where towering dawn redwoods, delicate Japanese maples, and ancient conifers create a canopy so lush it feels worlds away from the city below. For couples, this is a place to breathe deeply and simply *be together*.

Whether you arrive at golden hour when slanted light filters through the magnolia collection, or on a crisp autumn morning when the maples ignite in copper and crimson, the arboretum rewards slow, unhurried exploration. There's a quiet intimacy to walking these paths — no reservations required, no crowded dining rooms — just the crunch of leaves underfoot and the occasional distant call of birds. It's the kind of place that makes conversation feel effortless and silence feel comfortable.

### What to Expect

- **12+ miles of trails** ranging from gentle strolls to more immersive woodland walks
- **Dramatic seasonal color** — spring blooms and fall foliage are particularly breathtaking
- **A peaceful, unhurried atmosphere** ideal for long walks and genuine connection
- **Stunning views** toward Mount Hood and the surrounding Portland hills from select vantage points
- **Free admission**, making it an accessible choice for spontaneous or budget-conscious dates

### Perfect For

First dates, anniversaries, weekend morning escapes, nature-loving couples, and anyone craving a romantic retreat without leaving the city.
