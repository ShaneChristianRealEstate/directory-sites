## What to Look for in an Emergency Plumber

Not all plumbing companies are built for true emergencies. Here's what separates the best from the rest:

### 1. Genuine 24/7 Availability
Some companies advertise around-the-clock service but route calls to an answering machine after hours. Look for providers who answer live calls and can dispatch a technician the same night — not the next morning.

### 2. Licensed and Insured in Georgia
Always verify that your plumber holds a valid Georgia plumbing license and carries both general liability and workers' compensation insurance. This protects you if something goes wrong on the job.

### 3. Transparent, Upfront Pricing
Emergency plumbing often comes with after-hours fees, and that's fair — but you should know the cost before work begins. Reputable companies will give you a written estimate or clear explanation of charges before picking up a wrench.

### 4. Strong Local Reputation
Check Google reviews, the Better Business Bureau, and neighborhood forums like Nextdoor. A plumber with consistent five-star reviews and detailed feedback from Atlanta homeowners is far more reliable than one with a flashy website and little else.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring an Emergency Plumber

Before you hand over the job, take two minutes to ask these questions:

- **What is your estimated arrival time?** A true emergency plumber should be able to give you a realistic ETA — ideally within 60–90 minutes for urgent situations.
- **Are your technicians licensed and background-checked?** You're letting someone into your home, often in the middle of the night. You deserve to know who's showing up.
- **Do you charge extra for after-hours or weekend calls?** Understanding the fee structure upfront prevents unpleasant surprises on your invoice.
- **Do you offer a warranty on parts and labor?** Quality emergency plumbers stand behind their work with at least a short-term guarantee on repairs.

**What qualifies as a plumbing emergency?**
Burst or leaking pipes, sewage backups, no hot water in freezing temperatures, gas line issues, and overflowing toilets that can't be shut off all qualify as emergencies. When in doubt, it's always better to call — a professional can help you assess the urgency.

**How much does emergency plumbing cost in Atlanta, GA?**
