## What to Look for in an Emergency Plumber

### 1. True 24/7 Availability
Not every plumber who lists an emergency number actually answers it at 3 a.m. Look for companies that explicitly guarantee around-the-clock dispatch, not just an answering service that takes a message. Ask directly: *Who picks up the phone at midnight?*

### 2. Licensing and Insurance
In Texas, plumbers must be licensed through the Texas State Board of Plumbing Examiners (TSBPE). Always verify a plumber's license is current and that they carry both liability insurance and workers' compensation. This protects you if something goes wrong on the job.

### 3. Upfront, Transparent Pricing
Emergency work often comes with a premium, and that's fair — but you deserve to know the cost before work begins. Reputable emergency plumbers will provide a clear estimate or flat-rate pricing before picking up a wrench, even in urgent situations.

### 4. Local Knowledge and Reviews
A plumber familiar with San Antonio's infrastructure, local building codes, and common regional issues (like the freeze-related pipe bursts that followed Winter Storm Uri) is a real asset. Check Google reviews and the Better Business Bureau for consistent, recent feedback from your area.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

- **What is your guaranteed arrival window?** During a plumbing emergency, a vague "we'll be there soon" isn't good enough. Ask for a specific time frame.
- **Are all technicians licensed, or do you use subcontractors?** Knowing who shows up at your door matters for both quality and accountability.
- **Do you charge extra for nights, weekends, or holidays?** Emergency surcharges are common — just make sure you understand the full rate before committing.
- **Do you offer a warranty on parts and labor?** A confident, skilled plumber will stand behind their work with at least a short-term guarantee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How quickly can an emergency plumber reach me in San Antonio?**
Most reputable emergency plumbing services in San Antonio aim for a 30–90 minute response time, depending on your location within the metro area and current demand. During major weather events, expect longer waits and call as early as possible.

**What counts as a plumbing emergency?**
Situations that require immediate attention include burst or frozen pipes, sewage backups, gas line leaks, complete loss of water, and overflowing toilets that can't be stopped. If water is actively damaging your home, don't wait — call immediately.

**Is emergency plumbing service more expensive in San Antonio?**
After-hours and emergency calls typically carry higher service fees than standard daytime appointments. However, the cost of a timely repair almost always outweighs the expense of water damage repair, mold remediation, or structural repairs caused by delayed action.
