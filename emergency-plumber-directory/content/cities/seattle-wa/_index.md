## What to Look for in an Emergency Plumber

Choosing an emergency plumber under pressure is stressful. Knowing what matters most helps you make a confident decision fast.

### 1. True 24/7 Availability
Not every plumber who advertises "emergency service" actually answers the phone at 3 a.m. Look for companies that explicitly confirm round-the-clock availability, including weekends and holidays — and check recent reviews to verify they follow through.

### 2. Washington State Licensing and Insurance
Any legitimate plumber working in Seattle must hold a valid Washington State plumbing license and carry liability insurance. This protects you if something goes wrong during the repair. Don't hesitate to ask for license numbers and verify them through the Washington State Department of Labor & Industries.

### 3. Transparent Pricing and Upfront Estimates
Emergency plumbing can be expensive, but reputable companies will give you a clear estimate before work begins. Be cautious of vague quotes or businesses that refuse to discuss pricing until the job is done. Honest providers set expectations upfront — even in urgent situations.

### 4. Local Experience and Reputation
Seattle has unique plumbing challenges — aging sewer infrastructure, high rainfall, and homes ranging from 1920s Craftsmans to modern condos. A plumber with deep local experience understands these nuances. Look for consistent positive reviews on Google and the BBB, and pay attention to how businesses respond to negative feedback.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring an Emergency Plumber

Asking the right questions — even quickly — can save you headaches later:

- **"Can you give me an estimated arrival time and a cost range before you arrive?"** This sets realistic expectations and helps you compare options if needed.
- **"Is the technician coming out licensed and experienced with this type of issue?"** Some companies dispatch general handymen for overflow calls — you want a certified plumber.
- **"Do you guarantee your work, and what does that warranty cover?"** Reputable emergency plumbers stand behind their repairs with at least a short-term labor and parts warranty.
- **"Will my homeowner's insurance cover this, and can you help document the damage?"** Many emergency plumbers are experienced with insurance claims and can provide documentation to support your case.
