## What to Look for in an Emergency Plumber

### 1. True 24/7 Availability
Emergencies don't follow business hours. Look for providers who answer live calls around the clock — not just an answering machine with a promise of a callback. Ask specifically whether a licensed plumber (not just a dispatcher) is reachable at any hour.

### 2. Transparent, Upfront Pricing
Reputable emergency plumbers provide clear estimates before work begins, even after hours. Be cautious of vague quotes or companies that won't discuss pricing until the job is done. Flat-rate or clearly explained service fees are a strong sign of an honest operation.

### 3. Valid Licensing and Insurance
In Ohio, plumbers must be licensed through the state. Always verify that any company you hire carries both general liability insurance and workers' compensation. This protects your home and your wallet if something goes wrong on the job.

### 4. Fast Response Times
In a plumbing emergency, every minute counts. Look for local Columbus companies that advertise — and deliver — response times of 60 minutes or less. Local businesses with Columbus-based crews typically outperform large national chains when it comes to arrival speed.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

Before you give the go-ahead, it's worth taking two minutes to ask a few key questions:

- **"Are you licensed and insured in Ohio?"** Any reputable plumber will answer this confidently and be able to provide documentation.
- **"What are your after-hours service fees?"** Emergency calls often carry a premium — knowing that upfront prevents sticker shock on the invoice.
- **"Can you give me a written estimate before starting work?"** Verbal estimates can lead to disputes. A written quote protects both parties.
- **"How soon can someone be at my home?"** Response time is everything in an emergency, so get a specific window rather than a vague "as soon as possible."

Emergency plumbing calls in Columbus generally range from $150 to $500 or more, depending on the time of day, the complexity of the job, and travel time. After-hours and weekend calls typically carry a higher service fee. Always ask for a quote before authorizing work.

Situations like burst pipes, sewage backups, overflowing toilets that can't be stopped, no hot water in winter, and gas line concerns all qualify as emergencies. When in doubt, call — most Columbus plumbers can help you assess severity over the phone.
