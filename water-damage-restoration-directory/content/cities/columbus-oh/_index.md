## What to Look for in a Water Damage Restoration Company

### 1. IICRC Certification
The Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification (IICRC) sets the industry standard for water damage remediation. Look for companies whose technicians hold current IICRC credentials — this ensures they're trained in proper drying techniques, moisture detection, and safe mold prevention practices.

### 2. 24/7 Emergency Availability
Water damage compounds by the hour. A provider that offers genuine around-the-clock emergency response — not just an answering service — is critical. Confirm that technicians, not just dispatchers, are available at night and on weekends.

### 3. Direct Insurance Billing Experience
Navigating an insurance claim during a stressful situation is overwhelming. Companies experienced in working directly with insurance adjusters can simplify the documentation process and help ensure you receive a fair settlement.

### 4. Transparent Scope of Work
Reputable restoration companies will walk you through a clear assessment before work begins, explaining what needs to be done, why, and at what cost. Be cautious of vague estimates or pressure to sign contracts immediately.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Water Damage Restoration Company

Before you commit to a provider, ask these practical questions:

- **How quickly can you have a crew on-site?** Response time matters — ask for a specific timeframe, not just "as soon as possible."
- **Are your technicians IICRC-certified?** Certifications should apply to the people doing the work, not just the company name on the truck.
- **Do you handle mold remediation as well?** Water damage and mold often go hand in hand. Knowing whether you'll need a separate contractor later can save time and cost.
- **Can you communicate directly with my insurance company?** If so, ask for examples of how they've handled claims similar to yours.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does water damage restoration typically take in Columbus?**
Most residential water damage jobs take between three and five days for drying and initial remediation, though full restoration — including repairs and finishing work — can take one to two weeks depending on the severity.

**Does homeowner's insurance cover water damage in Ohio?**
It depends on the source. Sudden, accidental damage (like a burst pipe) is typically covered, while gradual damage from neglected maintenance or flooding from outside usually is not. Always review your specific policy and document damage thoroughly before work begins.

**Is mold a concern after water damage in Columbus?**
Yes. Columbus's humidity levels, particularly in summer months, can accelerate mold growth — sometimes within 24 to 48 hours of water intrusion. Prompt professional drying and moisture assessment are the best ways to prevent a secondary mold problem.
