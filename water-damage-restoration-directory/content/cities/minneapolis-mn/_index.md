## What to Look for in a Water Damage Restoration Provider

Choosing the right company is about more than picking the first result you find online. Here's what separates a truly reliable restoration team from the rest:

### 1. 24/7 Emergency Availability
Water damage gets worse by the hour. Mold can begin developing within 24–48 hours, and structural materials absorb moisture quickly. Look for companies that offer around-the-clock emergency response — not just a voicemail box, but an actual crew ready to deploy.

### 2. IICRC Certification
The Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification (IICRC) sets the industry standard for water damage mitigation. Certified technicians have demonstrated knowledge of proper drying techniques, moisture detection, and safe mold remediation practices.

### 3. Transparent Estimates and Insurance Coordination
A trustworthy company will provide a clear, written estimate before work begins and will work directly with your homeowner's or commercial insurance carrier. Avoid providers who pressure you into signing contracts before you've had a chance to review the scope of work.

### 4. Local Experience and Reviews
Companies like **Bedrock Restoration** and **Service Restoration** have built reputations specifically within the Minneapolis metro area. Local experience matters — these teams understand regional building codes, common local causes of water damage, and even the best timing windows for efficient drying given Minnesota's climate and humidity levels.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does water damage restoration typically take in Minneapolis?**
The timeline varies based on the severity of the damage. Minor incidents may be resolved in 3–5 days, while significant flooding or sewage backup can require 1–2 weeks or more for complete drying, remediation, and repairs.

**Does homeowner's insurance usually cover water damage restoration?**
Most standard homeowner's policies cover sudden and accidental water damage, like a burst pipe. However, flooding from external sources typically requires separate flood insurance. Always contact your insurer promptly and document all damage with photos before cleanup begins.

**Is mold always a concern after water damage?**
Mold growth is a serious risk whenever moisture is present for more than 24–48 hours. A professional restoration team will use moisture meters and thermal imaging to detect hidden dampness that could lead to mold, even behind walls or under flooring.
