## What to Look for in a Water Damage Restoration Company

### 1. Proper Licensing and Certifications
Always verify that a company holds a valid Texas contractor license and carries certifications from respected industry organizations like the IICRC (Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification). These credentials signal that technicians are trained to industry standards for water extraction, drying, and mold remediation.

### 2. 24/7 Emergency Availability
Water damage doesn't wait for business hours. Look for providers that offer around-the-clock emergency response and can realistically dispatch a crew to your location within one to two hours. Fast arrival dramatically reduces overall damage and restoration costs.

### 3. Transparent Estimates and Insurance Assistance
Reputable companies will provide clear, written estimates before any work begins. Even better, many top Houston providers work directly with insurance carriers and can help document damage, file claims, and navigate the paperwork — taking a major burden off your plate during a stressful time.

### 4. Proper Equipment and Proven Methods
Industrial-grade moisture meters, air movers, dehumidifiers, and thermal imaging cameras aren't optional extras — they're essential tools for a thorough job. Ask about the technology a company uses and whether they follow IICRC S500 standards for water damage restoration.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Water Damage Restoration in Houston

**How long does water damage restoration typically take in Houston?**
Most residential water damage jobs take anywhere from three to five days for drying alone, with full restoration potentially taking one to two weeks depending on the extent of the damage and whether mold remediation is required.

**Does Houston's humidity affect the restoration process?**
Yes, significantly. High ambient humidity slows the drying process, which is why professional-grade dehumidification equipment is especially important in the Houston area. DIY drying methods are rarely effective in this climate.

**Will my homeowner's insurance cover water damage restoration?**
Coverage depends on the source of the damage. Sudden and accidental water damage — like a burst pipe — is typically covered, while gradual leaks or flooding from outside (storm surge, overland flooding) usually require separate flood insurance. Your restoration provider can help document the damage for your claim.
