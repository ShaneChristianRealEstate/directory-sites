## What to Look for in a Water Damage Restoration Company

### 1. 24/7 Emergency Response
Water damage doesn't wait for business hours. Look for providers that offer around-the-clock emergency services and can typically arrive on-site within a few hours. Fast extraction and drying are the single biggest factors in preventing secondary damage and mold.

### 2. Proper Certifications and Licensing
Reputable companies carry certifications from the Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification (IICRC), the industry's gold standard. In Florida, restoration contractors should also hold the appropriate state licenses. Always verify credentials before signing anything.

### 3. Insurance Claim Assistance
Navigating a homeowner's insurance claim while your house is wet and stressful is no easy task. The best restoration companies have dedicated staff who can document damage thoroughly, communicate directly with your insurer, and help ensure you receive fair coverage for the work needed.

### 4. Transparent Estimates and Scope of Work
Get a written estimate that clearly outlines the scope of work, equipment to be used, and projected timeline. Be cautious of any company that pushes you to sign a broad authorization form before conducting a proper assessment.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Water Damage Restoration Company

- **How quickly can you arrive, and what does your initial assessment involve?** Understanding their response protocol tells you a lot about their professionalism and capacity.
- **Are your technicians IICRC-certified?** Certification ensures your team is trained in proper drying science, not just surface-level cleanup.
- **Do you handle mold testing and remediation as well?** In Orlando's humid environment, water damage and mold often go hand in hand. A company that can address both saves you from coordinating multiple contractors.
- **Can you provide references or reviews from recent local jobs?** Firsthand accounts from other Orlando homeowners are invaluable when assessing reliability and quality of work.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Water Damage Restoration in Orlando

**How long does water damage restoration typically take in Orlando?**
Most residential jobs take anywhere from three to seven days, depending on the extent of damage and how quickly drying can begin. Larger losses or cases involving mold remediation may take longer.

**Does homeowner's insurance cover water damage restoration in Florida?**
It depends on the cause. Sudden and accidental damage — like a burst pipe — is generally covered. Flood damage from storms typically requires separate flood insurance. Always review your policy and file promptly.

**Why is mold such a concern after water damage in Orlando?**
Orlando's heat and humidity create near-perfect conditions for mold growth, which can begin within 24 to 48 hours of water exposure. This is why fast response and thorough drying are critical — not just cosmetic cleanup.
